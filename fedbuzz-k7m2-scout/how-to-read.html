How to read the FedBuzz producer brief

This page explains the weekly filming brief Harris receives from Topic Scout. You do not need GitHub or RSS to use it.

Private: Do not share this page link, the brief link, or the RSS feed URL outside the Harris producer team.

Start here each week

  1. Open brief.html (same folder as this page).
  2. Read the numbered Skim — walkaround opens list first. These are the phone-camera opening lines.
  3. Expand only the topics you might film. You can ignore the rest.

What each topic block means

  • Walkaround line (heading) — the question or hook to say on camera first.
  • Consider filming if — a short, hedged take on why the topic might work. It is a suggestion, not an assignment.
  • Italic meta line — fit tag, source type, topic pillar, and how old the story is.
  • Read source — the news or thread the hook is based on. Check dates and facts before you film.
  • Other opens — alternate hooks (stakes, deadline, myth-bust) if the main line does not fit your voice.
  • Question answered — the viewer takeaway in one line.
  • Caution — filming or policy caveats (verify dates, avoid speculation, etc.).
  • Original headline & context (click to expand) — source headline, outlet, longer eval notes, and a text snippet.

Tags you might see

  • FDR — funnel / organic content. Hand-pick for those uses; not for planning retargeting pools.
  • pass / review — internal fit labels. Both can be filmable; use your judgment.

RSS vs this web brief

  • brief.html — best for reading in a browser (recommended for most producers).
  • feed.xml — for RSS apps (Feeder, etc.) if you prefer subscriptions. Same topics, different layout.

What this is not

  • Not a script. You still decide tone, length, and whether to film.
  • Not legal or HR advice. Harris QA notes flag items that need extra review when present.
